DATA PRIVACY AND PROTECTION POLICY

Version: 1.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Last Updated: January 1, 2026

1. PURPOSE

This Data Privacy and Protection Policy establishes guidelines for the collection, 
use, storage, and protection of personal and sensitive data.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all data collected, processed, or stored by the organization 
from customers, employees, partners, and third parties.

3. POLICY STATEMENT

3.1 Data Privacy Principles

The organization commits to:
- Collecting only necessary data
- Obtaining consent before data collection
- Protecting data from unauthorized access
- Providing transparency about data usage
- Allowing individuals to access their data

3.2 Data Collection

Personal data shall only be collected for:
- Legitimate business purposes
- Legal compliance
- Service delivery
- Customer support

Data subjects must be informed of:
- What data is collected
- Why it is collected
- How long it will be retained
- Who it may be shared with

3.3 Consent Management

Explicit consent is required for:
- Marketing communications
- Sharing data with third parties
- Processing sensitive personal data

Consent must be:
- Freely given
- Specific
- Informed
- Revocable at any time

3.4 Data Subject Rights

Individuals have the right to:
- Access their personal data
- Correct inaccurate data
- Request data deletion
- Object to data processing
- Data portability

Requests must be responded to within 30 days.

3.5 Data Retention

Personal data shall be retained only as long as necessary.
Retention periods are based on:
- Legal requirements
- Business needs
- Consent duration

Data must be securely deleted when no longer needed.

3.6 Third-Party Data Sharing

Data may only be shared with third parties when:
- Required by law
- Necessary for service delivery
- Explicitly consented by data subject

All third parties must sign Data Processing Agreements.

3.7 Data Breach Response

Data breaches must be:
- Detected and contained immediately
- Reported to management within 24 hours
- Notified to affected individuals if required
- Documented for regulatory compliance

3.8 International Data Transfers

Cross-border data transfers require:
- Adequacy decision or appropriate safeguards
- Standard contractual clauses
- Data subject notification

3.9 Employee Responsibilities

All employees must:
- Complete data privacy training annually
- Handle personal data with care
- Report suspected breaches immediately
- Follow data minimization principles

4. ENFORCEMENT

Violations may result in disciplinary action and regulatory penalties.

5. REFERENCES

- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- Organizational Code of Conduct

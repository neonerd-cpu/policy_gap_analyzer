SAMPLE INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (ISMS) POLICY

Version: 1.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Last Updated: January 1, 2026

1. PURPOSE

This Information Security Management System (ISMS) Policy establishes the framework for 
protecting organizational information assets and ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, 
and availability of data.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who have 
access to organizational information systems and data.

3. POLICY STATEMENT

3.1 Information Security Objectives

The organization is committed to:
- Protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access
- Ensuring business continuity
- Complying with applicable laws and regulations
- Maintaining customer trust

3.2 Roles and Responsibilities

The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is responsible for:
- Developing and maintaining information security policies
- Overseeing security operations
- Reporting security metrics to executive management

Department managers are responsible for:
- Ensuring compliance within their departments
- Reporting security incidents
- Participating in security awareness training

3.3 Access Control

All users must authenticate with unique credentials before accessing systems.
Access rights shall be reviewed quarterly.
Privileged access requires additional approval.

3.4 Data Classification

Information shall be classified as:
- Public: Information intended for public disclosure
- Internal: Information for internal use only
- Confidential: Sensitive business information
- Restricted: Highly sensitive information requiring strict controls

3.5 Acceptable Use

Users must:
- Use information systems only for authorized business purposes
- Protect their credentials
- Report suspicious activities
- Comply with all security policies

3.6 Security Awareness

All employees shall complete security awareness training upon hire and annually thereafter.

3.7 Physical Security

Physical access to data centers and server rooms is restricted to authorized personnel.
Visitors must be escorted at all times.

3.8 Change Management

All system changes must be documented, tested, and approved before implementation.

3.9 Monitoring and Logging

Security events shall be logged and reviewed regularly.
Logs shall be retained for at least 90 days.

3.10 Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated as needed.

4. ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

5. REFERENCES

- ISO/IEC 27001:2013
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- Organizational Code of Conduct
